What it handles

Works for any email-heavy role

Six categories the agent classifies and surfaces so the right person sees the right item at the right time.

Approvals and sign-offs

Emails requiring a decision or approval are flagged immediately — never buried under a Monday morning pile.

"Contract approval needed by EOD" surfaced in the 9am digest — actioned before 10am.

Escalations and urgencies

Client escalations, system alerts, and flagged issues are classified and surfaced regardless of when they arrive.

Client escalation at 7pm surfaces in the 9am next-day digest — first thing in the morning.

Time-sensitive replies

Emails that contain deadlines, meeting requests, or time-bounded asks are classified as high-priority automatically.

"Can we confirm by 3pm?" flagged in midday digest with full context.

Direct asks from key contacts

Configure a VIP list. Any email from a named contact is always surfaced, regardless of the content.

Board member email always appears in digest — even if it looks like a routine update.

Noise filtered out

Newsletters, marketing, auto-replies, and CC-only emails are removed before the digest. You never see them in Slack.

47 emails received on Monday. 3 surfaced to Slack. 44 filtered. All 44 still in Outlook, untouched.

Reply tracking

Once you reply from Outlook, the task auto-closes in the Slack digest. No manual cleanup, no double-handling.

Replied to the contract email in Outlook at 10:15am. Digest item auto-resolved by 10:16am.
